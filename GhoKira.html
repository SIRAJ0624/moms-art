Gho and Kira: The Traditional Dress of Bhutan

There was a time when you could often tell where a person was from by looking at their outfits, but with modernisation, traditional attires are slowly becoming something of the past. In Bhutan however, the traditional dress of Gho for men and Kira for women remains alive and culturally significant.

Bhutanese traditional attire is not only colorful and eye-catching, but also holds immense cultural value. These clothes are expressions of Bhutanese identity, not just fabrics sewn together.

On any ordinary day, you will see Bhutanese people walking down the street or entering workplaces gracefully dressed in their traditional attire.

The Bhutanese Traditional Dress for Men

The Gho

Men in Bhutan wear a knee-length robe called the Gho, wrapped around the body and tied with a belt. It forms a pouch often referred to as “the largest pocket in the world.” It is worn daily, especially by those in schools, colleges, or government sectors.

The Bhutanese Traditional Dress for Women

The Kira

Bhutanese women wear an ankle-length dress called the Kira, which is fastened at the shoulders with traditional brooches (Koma). It can also be worn as a half-length skirt.

Kiras are traditionally handwoven using raw silk with intricate patterns. On special occasions, women wear brightly colored and complex Kiras, while daily wear involves simpler and lighter versions. Fashion-forward Bhutanese women often accessorize their Kiras with stylish blouses, jewelry, and matching footwear.
